Suffering is the most fundamental aspect of humanity. It is the first emotion we learn to react to by crying when we are hungry, tired, or covered in shit. It is the one emotion we let guide us our whole lives as a "gut feeling". If you are uncomfortable and suffering, then you become motivated to change your situation. This works the whole spectrum of situations from adjusting your sitting position all the way to how nations are governed.

Listening to my suffering is mindfulness. Having a trusting relationship with my uncomfort is how I grow and better.


## Shoes

My whole life I have worn shoes that offer nearly no support at all. At first they were just a fashion statement as all my friends wore Chuck Taylors. It became part of me because there was little else to me than what I looked like as a child. But as I grew into my own I started to think about my feet.

My feet hurt.

My feet are not Chuck Taylor shaped. Not even the extra wide ones. I had been cramming my feet into them for years because that is who I was, I thought. It was so important to me that I even considered getting surgery to remove my pinky toes and thus solve my problem!

I was willing to mutilate myself to keep this symbol of me instead of simply listening to my feet and be me.

## Walking

Being an American with an Office Job, I sit a lot. Once upon a time, I was a competitive gamer who would sit for hours. I had a special pad to put on my chair so I could sit longer. This was okay because I was relatively active. I would otherwise walk and run all the time for fun and for school. Then school ended and I gained a hundred pounds.

Hiking was how I kept up as much as I could. I would start speed hiking. Not quite running, just minimizing my kit so much so that I didn't enjoy camping and wanted to enjoy the walking. During my peak I was doing just over 30 miles a day at a respectable but not crazy pace. I would just walk for 15 hours a day. Stop long enough to eat a tortilla covered in peanutbutter and tuna, and keep walking.

Since the focus of my hiking was the walk itself, I was very mindful of my relationship to the earth. You become attuned to the path with enough time and every change in soil texture, hardness, or level becomes an intimate change. It was as if someone you desire and see every day changed their hair or they're dressed up for an occasion. You don't desire them more you just become re-awakened to their beauty. A new curve, a new angle, a new scent on the wind. 

Nothing is less sexy than being uncomfortable while with your love, but my relationship with the Path has always been complex. 

Every hike sucks for the first mile or so. I'm sure I just needed to learn to stretch or something, but just being with the Path filled me with lust and precations fell to the way side. This was an acceptable exchange for intimacy with the Path, but once I was with them I could find my comfort. This is where the mindfulness kicked in, I had to listen to my legs, my back, my arms, everything and try new positions and rhythms until it felt great to be with them again.

This mindfulness practice was much more complicated than the shoes. It was a long term relationship with me in the here and now, the paths of Kentucky I had fallen for, and the me of the future who desperately didn't want back problems.
